Musk Loses OpenAI Lawsuit! Ziwei Doushu Analyzes the Power Struggle Between Seven Kill and Emperor Star

In May 2026, a US federal jury formally dismissed Elon Musk’s lawsuit against OpenAI and its CEO Sam Altman, closing one of the most closely watched power struggles in the tech world. Musk charged in with the ferocity of the Seven Kill (Qi Sha) star, only to fall before the might of the Emperor (Zi Wei) star. In Ziwei Doushu, the outcome of this legal battle was written in the stars long before the first gavel fell. Which star rules your life palace — and what does it say about your role in the game of power?

The Seven Kill Star: The Disruptor’s Power to Break Through

The Seven Kill (Qi Sha) star is the sixth star of the South Dipper, belonging to Geng Metal. It governs destruction, innovation, and transformation — earning the title “General Star.” People with Seven Kill in their Life Palace are decisive, fearless, and relentlessly action-oriented. Elon Musk, as a serial entrepreneur who upended the automotive, aerospace, and payment industries, embodies pure Seven Kill energy — challenging established order and conquering through disruptive innovation.

However, the essence of Seven Kill lies in the “Sha-Po-Lang” (Kill-Break-Greed) dynamic. While its offensive power is immense, it lacks stamina for prolonged battles. When Seven Kill natives initiate lawsuits or power challenges, they charge in with overwhelming momentum — but if they cannot secure a swift victory, they risk losing as their energy wanes. Musk’s defeat in this lawsuit is a textbook example of a Seven Kill offensive colliding with an unbreachable defense.

The Emperor Star: The Sovereign’s Overwhelming Authority

The Emperor Star (Zi Wei) is the sovereign star of the North Dipper, belonging to Ji Earth. Those with Zi Wei in their Life Palace are born with leadership authority, strategic vision, and the ability to integrate resources. Sam Altman’s role in shaping OpenAI’s governance structure perfectly illustrates Zi Wei’s archetype — building systems, commanding influence, and marshaling diverse resources toward a unified goal.

Zi Wei natives rarely initiate conflicts, but when challenged, the entire bureaucratic system behind them — Tian Fu (Treasury, the armory), Tian Xiang (Minister, the seal of authority) — activates automatically. The higher the Zi Wei configuration, the more formidable the legal and public relations resources at their disposal. In court, they are nearly unassailable. This verdict perfectly demonstrates the Emperor Star’s supreme strategy: “win by not losing.”

Key Stars in Power Struggles: Lian Zhen and Tian Fu

▎Lian Zhen (Integrity Star): The Barometer of Litigation Luck

Lian Zhen, known as the “Imprisonment Star” in Ziwei Doushu, governs law, contracts, regulations, and lawsuits. Your performance in legal proceedings depends heavily on whether Lian Zhen is temple-bright (miao wang) or weak in your birth chart. Those with Lian Zhen well-positioned in the Yin or Shen palaces and free from negative influences have clear legal thinking, precise strategy, and excel at wielding the law as a shield. Conversely, a weakened or afflicted Lian Zhen leaves one vulnerable and reactive in litigation.

▎Tian Fu (Treasury Star): The Safe Harbor in Power Struggles

Tian Fu, the Treasury Star, represents accumulated resources and defensive capability. In power struggles, those with Tian Fu in the Life Palace or Career Palace possess ample financial reserves and relationship capital to endure protracted battles. When Zi Wei and Tian Fu occupy the same palace — the “Zi Fu Tong Gong” configuration — the power foundation is virtually unshakeable against external forces.
