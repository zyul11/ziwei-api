Life Palace & Career Palace: The Gentle Power of Tian Tong and Tai Yin

The subject's Life Palace (Ming Gong) is in Hai, with Tian Tong (Heavenly Harmony) and Tai Yin (Great Yin) as the main stars — both Water element stars, giving her a gentle, delicate personality rich in artistic sensibility and empathy. Tian Tong is a Fortune Star, representing stability and harmony, ideal for work requiring care and communication. Tai Yin governs inner emotion and aesthetics, excelling at detail and planning. Her Career Palace (Shi Ye Gong) is in Mao, with Tian Ji (Heavenly Opportunity) as the main star. Tian Ji is a Wood element star symbolizing wisdom and change, particularly suited for careers involving thinking, analysis, or planning. This combination suggests she would thrive in education, psychological counseling, art and design, or cultural creative industries. For example, she could be an outstanding children's picture book author, healing hearts through gentle stories — or an interior designer, weaving aesthetics into everyday life. The key is choosing an industry that blends emotion with rationality, rather than purely competitive fields.

Wealth Palace & Four Transformations: Decoding Wealth Through Hua Lu, Hua Quan, Hua Ke, and Hua Ji

The subject's Wealth Palace (Cai Bo Gong) is in Wei, with Tai Yang (Sun) as the main star, You Bi (Right Assistant) as the assisting star, and both Hua Quan (Power Transformation) and Hua Ke (Celebrity Transformation) appearing simultaneously. Tai Yang represents brightness and generosity. Hua Quan grants her leadership ability in her career, while Hua Ke elevates her reputation and professional image. You Bi is an assisting star, suggesting she can accumulate wealth through networking and collaboration. However, the Wealth Palace also contains Huo Xing (Mars/Fire Star), indicating potential impatience and conflict in the process of earning money. Her career direction should center on "serving others" — for instance, non-profit management, educational training, or brand consulting. These industries allow Tai Yang's warmth to shine while building authority through Hua Quan and Hua Ke. Additionally, Tian Ji Hua Lu (Prosperity Transformation) in her Career Palace means wisdom and creativity can generate stable income, but speculation should be avoided — deep cultivation of a professional field is key. For example, if she runs a small cultural creative studio focused on handicraft teaching, she can achieve both income and fulfillment.

Travel Palace & Social Palace: Opportunities in Your Network

The Travel Palace (Qian Yi Gong) is in Si, with Wu Qu (Military Music) as the main star and Tian Xiang (Heavenly Seal) as the assistant, forming the "Wu Xiang" combination — indicating decisiveness and coordination skills when developing outside. Wu Qu is a Wealth Star, suitable for finance, management, or technical fields. Tian Xiang emphasizes service and regulation, fitting for legal or civil service careers. If she pursues international trade, financial planning, or corporate consulting, she will thrive. The Social Palace (Jiao You Gong) is in You, with Po Jun (Destroyer) as the main star and no assistants. Po Jun represents revolution and innovation, suggesting her friends are mostly entrepreneurs or industry pioneers who can offer fresh career advice. For example, she might connect with someone in a tech startup who introduces her to the AI education field, leading to a successful career pivot. Overall, she should actively participate in career workshops and networking events in Taipei — her Travel and Social Palaces work together, making her network a critical breakthrough point for her career.

Fortune Palace & Health Palace: Inner Drive and Health Warnings

The Fortune Palace (Fu De Gong) is in Chou, with Zi Wei (Emperor) and Tian Fu (Great Treasury) as the main stars, forming the "Zi Fu" pattern — meaning she seeks stability and authority in her inner world, suited for long-term planning industries like senior management or academic research. Zi Wei is the Imperial Star, granting her leadership qualities. Tian Fu emphasizes treasury and storage, fitting for finance or resource management. The Health Palace (Ji E Gong) is in Xu, with Qi Sha (Seven Killings) as the main star and no assistants. Qi Sha represents pressure and change, reminding her to watch her cardiovascular health and avoid an overly intense work pace. Her optimal career path is gradually transitioning from executor to manager — for instance, advancing from teacher to dean of studies, or from designer to creative director. This aligns with the stability of the Zi Fu pattern while reducing the toil brought by Qi Sha. Taipei's fast-paced work environment means she should plan a short break every quarter to recharge both mind and career.
